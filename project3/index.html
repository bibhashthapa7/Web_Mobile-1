About Nepal:

Mountains:

Mount Everest is the most well-known mountain in Nepal due to its status as the world's tallest peak. However, Nepal has many additional mountains. The mountains of Nepal beckon superlatives, from the tallest to the most popular to the most remote. Nepal, which is located on the souther slopes of the Himalayas, is home to eight of the world's ten highest peaks. These peaks include Mount Everest (8,848m; on the border between Nepal and Tibet) which is also the tallest mountain of the world, Lhotse (8,516 m), Makalu (8,463 m), and Kanchenjunga (8,586 m).

Rivers:

Nepal is well-known for being the source of the Ganges River. Many of the Ganges' major tributaries originate in Nepal. The Ganges has four feeder rivers: the Kankai, Koshi, Bagmati, and Gandaki. The first group of rivers  include the country's three main river systems, the Koshi, Gandaki, and Karnali, all of which originate in glaciers and snow-fed lakes. Rivers in the second group include the Mechi, Mahakali, Bagmati, Kamala, Rapti, and others, the majority of which originate in the Mahabharat range.

Cities:

Nepal has numerous large cities, which contribute to the country's overall population of approximately 31 million people. Kathmandu is by far the largest city in Nepal. It is not only the largest city in Nepal, with a population of 1.4 million, but it is also the only city in Nepal with a population that has reached the 1,000,000 mark. Pokhara, the next largest city, with a population of only 200,000 people, a far cry from the figures stated in Nepal. There are five additional cities with populations greater than 100,000. There are also other smaller cities with large populations, with populations exceeding 10,000 people each city.

Temples:

Nepal has a plethora of religious architectural wonders due to its ideal combination of Hinduism and Buddhism. The temples in Nepal are a must-see while visiting the nation, with some having a deep connection to the Ramayana and others just stunning in their grandeur. The Swayambhunath Stupa, Boudhanath Stupa, Changunarayan Temple, Budhanilkantha, Dakshinkali, Pashupatinath, and Manakamana are some religious sites one must not miss while visiting the country.

Places to go:
Kathmandu Kathmandu, Nepal's largest city, serves as both the country's governmental and cultural center. Kathmandu is a city where old traditions coexist with cutting-edge technology. The grandeur of the past, on the other hand, enchants the visitor, who may linger on an elegantly carved wooden window frame, an 18th century bronze sculpture, or a spiritually uplifting temple or stupa.
Patan Patan, or 'Lalitpur,' the city of artists, is located 5 kilometers southeast of Kathmandu and is home to the valley's best craftsmen who have retained traditional skills such as repoussé and the lost wax process used to create stunning sculptures. With its tiny lanes, brick residences, and a plethora of well-preserved Hindu temples, Buddhist monasteries (vihars), and monuments, the city preserves much of its original beauty. The tinkering of craftsmen hunched over the statuettes they are molding is the most audible sound in Patan.
Bhaktapur Bhaktapur or Bhadgaon, meaning the City of Devotees, is a popular tourist site that transports tourists back in time. Bhaktapur is located 12 kilometers east of Kathmandu on the Arniko Highway. Bhaktapur, has managed to maintain its unique ambience with brick-paved roads, attractive red brick homes, and a way of life that dates back to medieval times. This historic city is particularly well-known for its ceramics and woodcarving, which are prominently displayed on the squares and windows of local buildings and temples.
Kirtipur  Kirtipur, a historic Newar settlement, is a small hill village with paved lanes flanked with traditional red-brick buildings with tiled roofs and temple spaces. Its age-old traditional Newari architecture combining with the new is an experience in and of itself, and one you won't want to miss. Kirtipur is a vibrant town that conducts a variety of traditional festivals called as Jatras in Nepali, which visitors may attend. Indrayani Jatra, held in November and December, and Bagh Bhairab Jatra, held in August, are the most well-known Jatras.
Pokhara Pokhara's clean air, beautiful background of snowy peaks, azure lakes, and surrounding vegetation have earned it the title of "the gem in the Himalaya," a region of exceptional natural beauty. Pokhara, with the gorgeous Annapurna range as a backdrop, is an excellent choice for both a weekend trip and a lengthy relaxing vacation. Pokhara Valley, the entrance to the Annapurna area, where many a trekker finds his Shangri-la, is high on the list of "must-see" destinations in Nepal.
Lumbini Lumbini is the birthplace of the Buddha and one of the world's most important spiritual places, attracting Buddhist pilgrims from all over the world. Today, you may visit approximately twenty-five Buddhist monasteries established by various countries ranging from Vietnam to France, learn Buddhism, meditate, and explore the mystical Mayadevi Gardens.
