COVID-19 and its impact on the US Economy

Impact on: Housing

The combination of increased demand and limited supply has resulted in an unprecedented rise in house prices. The median listing price increased by 18.7 percent year over year, marking 35 consecutive weeks of double-digit price rise, and the total number of properties actively listed for sale remains less than half of what it was before the recession.

The Household Pulse figures also reveal that millions of people are behind on their rent or mortgage payments. According to statistics collected September 15-27, an estimated 10.3 million individuals living in a rental property — 14 percent of adult renters — were behind on their rent. Some of these households were already behind on their payments before the pandemic, while many others faced economic shocks such as job loss, reduced hours, or the death of a family member. The majority of the overdue loans, representing 2.1 million borrowers, were more than three months past due, indicating significant economic difficulty. Furthermore, more than one in four renters with incomes under $25,000 reported that they were behind on their rent.
